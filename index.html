About

I work on graph deep learning. My research focuses on building neural network models that operate on graph-structured data — learning representations of nodes, edges, and whole graphs in ways that respect their underlying combinatorial structure. I'm broadly interested in the foundations of graph neural networks (GNNs), the mathematics behind geometric deep learning, and how these methods transfer to scientific and real-world problems.

I care about doing useful, well-grounded work. I'm drawn to questions where a sharper theoretical understanding of a model leads to a measurably better tool — better expressivity, better generalization, better calibration. I try to keep my code clean and my experiments reproducible.

Research

My research centers on graph machine learning with an emphasis on scalable temporal graph methods, mathematical foundations, and high-impact applications. I focus on research that is both theoretically grounded and practical on real-world data.

Temporal graph learning at scale
Developing efficient methods for label prediction on temporal knowledge graphs and temporal heterogeneous graphs, including large-scale settings motivated by EdgeScore.
Mathematics of graph learning
Studying the mathematical structure behind graph neural networks and geometric deep learning to improve model reliability, expressivity, and generalization.
Human-centered graph AI systems
Applying graph learning in operational environments, including route prediction for shuttle cars in room-and-pillar coal mines.
Synthetic relational data generation
Exploring hybrid pipelines where GNNs generate relational structure and ensemble ML models generate row-level attributes for realistic synthetic datasets.
Knowledge graphs and semantic AI infrastructure
Investigating the intersection of knowledge graphs, semantic layers, and agentic AI systems for enterprise-scale reasoning and trustworthy analytics.
