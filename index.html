About

I work on graph deep learning. My research focuses on building neural network models that operate on graph-structured data — learning representations of nodes, edges, and whole graphs in ways that respect their underlying combinatorial structure. I'm broadly interested in the foundations of graph neural networks (GNNs), the theory of geometric deep learning, and how these methods transfer to scientific and real-world problems.

I care about doing useful, well-grounded work. I'm drawn to questions where a sharper theoretical understanding of a model leads to a measurably better tool — better expressivity, better generalization, better calibration. I try to keep my code clean and my experiments reproducible.

Research

My current work sits at the intersection of the topics below. If any of these overlap with your interests, I'd be glad to hear from you.

Graph neural networks
Message-passing architectures, attention on graphs, and higher-order / subgraph methods. Understanding what these models can and cannot represent.
Expressivity & theory
Connections between GNNs and the Weisfeiler–Leman hierarchy, positional and structural encodings, and the limits of message passing.
Geometric & equivariant models
Learning on data with symmetry — group-equivariant networks for molecules, point clouds, and physical systems.
Scalability & large graphs
Training and inference on web-scale graphs: sampling, sparsification, and efficient architectures.
Applications
Molecular property prediction, knowledge graphs, and other domains where structure carries real information.
